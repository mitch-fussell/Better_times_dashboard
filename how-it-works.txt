BETTERTIMES DASHBOARD — HOW IT WORKS
====================================

What it's for
-------------
It answers one question a coloured spreadsheet can't easily: "Which clients
haven't we spoken to lately, and who recently had a problem?" Instead of
scrolling a giant grid, the dashboard tells you who needs attention.


Signing in
----------
The dashboard lives on a web page. Everyone on the team has their own login
(email + password), so you can see who did what. You can change your name or
your password any time from the menu in the top-right corner.


The two main screens
--------------------
1. The home screen ("attention queue")
   A simple list of clients, sorted by who needs contact most. Clients you've
   gone too long without speaking to (or who recently raised an issue) rise to
   the top. This is the screen to start your day on.

2. The calendar
   The familiar client-by-day grid. Each coloured square is a logged contact:
     - Green  = Proactive  (you reached out first)
     - Red    = Tickets    (the client came to you with an issue)
     - Yellow = Onboarding  (early-stage contact)
   You can scroll through dates and clients, and filter to one type or one
   client at a time.


Logging a check-in
------------------
Click any square on the calendar (or the "Log check-in" button at the top),
pick the client, the type, and the date — done. The dashboard updates straight
away. Hover over a square to see what was logged and who logged it.


Who did what
------------
Every check-in records the name of the person who logged it. Hover a square, or
open its menu, and you'll see "Proactive by Mitch", for example. That's why
everyone has their own login.


Categories
----------
Green / Red / Yellow are the built-in types, but you can add your own (with your
own colour) using "Manage categories" on the calendar.


Where the information lives
---------------------------
Everything is stored securely online, not on any one computer. That means the
whole team always sees the same up-to-date picture, from anywhere, and nothing
is lost if a laptop breaks. Only people with a login can see or change anything.


In short
--------
Log your client contacts as you go, glance at the home screen to see who's been
neglected, and use the calendar for the full picture. The spreadsheet days are
over.
